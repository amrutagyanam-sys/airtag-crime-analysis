Case Study: AirTag Crime Analysis

A deep-dive into how Apple AirTags are misused for stalking, vehicle theft, and covert tracking.

Scope & Questions

Research Focus

This case study analyzes Apple AirTags as a dual-use IoT tracking technology. The goal is to understand:

  • How AirTags technically work (Bluetooth LE, U1, Find My network).
  • How attackers repurpose them for stalking and auto theft.
  • Where current anti-stalking protections fail victims.
  • What design, policy, and user-level changes can reduce harm.

The project combines technical analysis, behavioral research on stalking, and real-world case reports into one cohesive security narrative.

Tech & Threats

Key Technical & Criminal Insights

  • AirTags lack GPS but leverage a crowdsourced Apple device mesh for location relay.
  • Attackers can modify firmware, remove speakers, or clone identifiers for silent, persistent tracking.
  • Cross-platform gaps: iOS users get better alerts than Android users, creating asymmetric victim protection.
  • Auto theft operations attach tags to vehicles in public areas and use them to track cars home.

The output is structured as a whitepaper, briefing deck, and can be easily adapted into SOC playbooks, training content, or awareness material.
